predict-peptide-ions

Description:

Given a peptide and a charge state, predict the ions according to the provided options. The program will be used primarily for validating the ion prediction code, and it's main functionality will ultimately be wrapped in search-spectrum.

Usage:

predict-peptide-ions [options]  <peptide sequence>  <charge state>

Input:

  • <peptide sequence> – The literal peptide sequence (e.g. EAMAPK) that is used to predict the ions.
  • <charge state> – The charge state of the peptide used to predict the ions.
Output:

    The program prints to standard output a series of header lines, describing how the ions were predicted. 
      # PEPTIDE: <peptide sequence>
  # CHARGE: <peptide charge>
    And then the predicted ions, in the following format: 
      <m/z> \t <mass> \t <ion-charge-state> \t <primary-ion> \t <peptide-bond-index> \t <neutral-loss> \n
    Where 
      <m/z> is the ion's mass-to-charge
  <mass> is the ion's (charged) mass
  <charge-state> is the ion's charge e.g. 1,2,3
  <ion-series> is one of (b,y,p)
  <bond-index> is in [1...n), where n is peptide length
  <neutral-loss> is one of (none|nh3|co|h2o)
Options:
  • [--primary-ions b|y|by] – Predict the specified primary ion series. 'b' indicates b-ions only, 'y' indicates y-ions only, 'by' indicates both. (Possibly add additional ions-series later ('a', 'c', etc.)? (Default=by)
  • [--precursor-ion T|F] – Predict the precursor ions, and all associated ions (neutral-losses, multiple charge states) consistent with the other specified options.
  • [--neutral-losses none|h2o|co|nh3|all] – Predict the the specified neutral loss peaks ('none' indicates no neutral losses, 'h2o' indicates water loss only, 'co' indicates a-ions only, 'nh3' indicates ammonia losses only, 'all' indicates all three kinds of losses). (Default=all)
  • [--max-ion-charge 1|2|3|peptide] – Predict ions up to this charge-state. The integer options ('1','2' and '3') specify a fixed maximum charge-state. The 'peptide' option indicates that the ions should range up to the maximum charge-state of the peptide itself (thus, a +2 charge state peptide would have ions of +1 and +2). (Default=peptide)
Warning messages:
  • FATAL: The sequence "peptide" is not a valid peptide sequence.
  • FATAL: The value "charge" is not a valid charge state.
Bugs:
  • predict-peptide-ions does not have bugs, it has features.
